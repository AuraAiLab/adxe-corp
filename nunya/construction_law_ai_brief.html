Department 01

Litigation & Dispute Resolution

The ProblemWhat AI DoesThe Blocker
Problem
Document review eats associate hours
50,000–200,000 docs per dispute. Associates spend weeks tagging and summarizing at rates that lose pitches to flat-fee competitors.
AI Fix
Reads the whole binder overnight
AI flags relevant docs, summarizes facts, and surfaces contradictions between daily logs and claim narratives. Three weeks becomes three days.
Blocker
Client data security anxiety
Partners worry about uploading client files. Fix: private deployment — AI runs inside your firewall. One IT decision, not a tech overhaul.
Problem
Timeline reconstruction is manual
Proving delay claims means rebuilding a project timeline from scattered emails and logs — done in spreadsheets, by hand, at partner rates.
AI Fix
Builds the chronology from the record
AI extracts every date-stamped event, maps it to contract milestones, and flags gaps a human misses at document 8,000.
Blocker
"Our files are too messy"
Most project records arrive as chaotic PDF dumps. AI handles exactly this. The blocker is perception, not reality.
Department 02

Contract Drafting & Review

The ProblemWhat AI DoesThe Blocker
Problem
Inconsistent clause language across projects
Different associates draft different versions of the same clause. Partners spend hours on quality control that should be standardized.
AI Fix
Checks every contract against your playbook
AI reviews incoming contracts against your firm's preferred positions and drafts your redlines. Partners review exceptions, not every line.
Blocker
The playbook doesn't exist in writing
Preferred positions live in senior partners' heads. AI is the forcing function that finally makes someone document them. That's a management step, not a tech step.
Problem
Subcontract flow-down review is error-prone
Florida law requires careful flow-down of prime contract terms. Getting indemnification wrong creates risk that surfaces years later.
AI Fix
Cross-references prime and sub contracts
Upload both. AI identifies every term that should flow down, checks whether it did, flags gaps, and drafts correction language. Two hours to fifteen minutes.
Blocker
Trust in AI accuracy on legal language
AI here is a first-pass tool — the partner still signs off. You're eliminating the tedious read, not the judgment call.
Department 03

Claims & Change Orders

The ProblemWhat AI DoesThe Blocker
Problem
Nobody documented it in real time
Verbal instructions, changed conditions, and owner-directed acceleration go unrecorded. When the claim comes, the record is thin and reconstruction is expensive.
AI Fix
AI-assisted daily field documentation
Simple mobile tools prompt supers to capture the right information daily. AI structures the input into legally defensible contemporaneous records. Prevention beats reconstruction.
Blocker
This serves clients — so who pays?
Best path: bundle it into a project monitoring retainer. Anyone who has survived one bad claim will pay for this immediately.
Problem
Damages calculations cost too much
Pricing a delay or disruption claim requires experts at $400–600/hour for work that is largely data processing, not judgment.
AI Fix
AI handles the data processing layer
AI ingests payroll records, equipment logs, and schedule updates and produces a preliminary damages model your expert reviews — not builds from scratch. Cut expert hours 40–60%.
Blocker
Expert resistance & Daubert exposure
The expert's role shifts from data processor to methodology validator. The audit trail is actually cleaner. Bar guidance is still catching up on this one.
Department 04

Research & Knowledge Management

The ProblemWhat AI DoesThe Blocker
Problem
Institutional knowledge walks out the door
Senior attorneys carry years of experience — preferred approaches, judicial tendencies, lessons from past cases — in their heads. When they leave, it goes with them.
AI Fix
AI-searchable firm knowledge base
Past briefs, memos, and analyses are indexed. A junior associate can ask what was argued in the last delay claim before a specific judge and get the answer in seconds.
Blocker
Files aren't organized and nobody wants to
AI handles messy files, but someone has to connect it to the firm's document system. The technical piece is easy. Getting partners to agree on a system is harder.
Problem
Florida construction law research is a time sink
Chapter 713, §558 notice requirements, prompt payment statutes — these change and interact. Associates spend hours confirming what they think they know.
AI Fix
Legal research assistant trained on Florida law
Lexis+ AI and Thomson Reuters CoCounsel draft research memos in minutes from verified sources. The attorney validates. Research time drops 50–70% on standard questions.
Blocker
Fear of hallucinated case citations
Addressed in purpose-built legal AI tools that cite only verified sources. The key: use legal-specific AI, not general AI. Most firms don't know the distinction exists.
Department 05

Operations & Business Development

The ProblemWhat AI DoesThe Blocker
Problem
Client intake is slow and inconsistent
Conflicts checks, engagement letters, retainer collection — handled differently by every partner, sometimes sloppily, with malpractice exposure under deadline pressure.
AI Fix
Automated intake workflow
Client fills a structured form. Conflicts check runs automatically. Engagement letter is drafted from the intake data. Partner approves and signs. Admin work disappears.
Blocker
Practice management software fragmentation
Clio, MyCase, legacy systems — none connected to AI out of the box. Integration is a one-time setup, not a major IT project. Usually two to four weeks once someone decides.
Problem
RFP responses require senior time they shouldn't
Nobody else knows the firm's relevant experience well enough to write it, so partners spend a day on proposals that should take an hour.
AI Fix
AI proposal drafting from firm history
AI trained on past proposals and matter descriptions drafts the RFP response. Partner reviews and personalizes. A day becomes an hour, and quality goes up.
Blocker
No single source of truth for firm experience
If matter history isn't documented anywhere, AI has nothing to draw from. Fixing the knowledge management problem once fixes intake, proposals, and research simultaneously.

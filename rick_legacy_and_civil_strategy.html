Track One

Civil engineering solutions & advisory

This track is about using Rick’s judgment, experience, and industry credibility to help shape practical solutions for civil engineering and heavy construction teams. He should not be used as an AI pitchman. He should be used as an industry translator, standards filter, and advisory authority.

What we need from him

  • His honest view of where firms lose time, money, trust, and control.
  • What senior leaders actually care about versus what vendors think they care about.
  • Where documentation breaks down across projects, claims, RFIs, submittals, and executive reporting.
  • What would feel practical and respectable to a civil firm instead of gimmicky.
  • Feedback on proposed features, workflows, dashboards, and knowledge systems.
  • Language that sounds right to real principals, builders, executives, and engineers.

What we can build from it

  • Civil Knowledge System — answers over specs, drawings, RFIs, submittals, contracts, and correspondence.
  • Executive Project Pulse — weekly risk, change-order, delay, and commitment visibility for leaders.
  • Institutional Knowledge Capture — preserve senior knowledge before it leaves the firm.
  • Documentation & Dispute Readiness — structure project records so firms are less exposed and more organized.
  • Operational Workflow Tools — meeting capture, issue logging, commitment tracking, and structured project memory.
01 How this should work Use Rick as an industry advisor, translator, and standards filter.
Advisory model +

Rick’s best role is to evaluate, refine, and reality-check ideas. He can help answer: Would a serious firm buy this? Who owns this problem? What would feel risky? What would make this feel useful? What part of this is real, and what part is just technology excitement?

The goal is to use his lived understanding of civil engineering and heavy construction to shape products that are grounded in actual operating pain rather than abstract AI concepts.

  • A list of high-trust use cases ADXE can actually pursue.

Track Three

Where the two tracks collide

The combined project is where the memoir and the business strategy meet. The point is not to commercialize his life. The point is to preserve his life fully and allow the professional wisdom inside it to inform something useful for the next generation.

Combined project statement

Preserve Rick Espino’s life, values, stories, and hard-won judgment in a way that honors his family, records his contribution to the profession, and creates a grounded foundation for building practical systems for civil engineering firms.

Personal gain

He gets to see, hear, and feel the full weight of what he built — not just in concrete and steel, but in family, trust, mentorship, and love.

Professional gain

You gain a real industry lens for shaping ADXE offerings around information friction, operational visibility, documentation, and institutional knowledge.

Best guiding principle: start with preservation and respect. Let the business value come out of the wisdom naturally.
